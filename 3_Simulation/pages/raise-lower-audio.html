üîó Why This Comes After Slip Audio

In a professional editing workflow, audio functions are ordered intentionally. Raise & Lower Audio is placed after the Slip Audio function for a critical reason:

‚úÇÔ∏è 1. Cut/Trim Set clip boundaries
‚Üí
üîÄ 2. Slip Audio Align the right content
‚Üí
üîä 3. Raise/Lower Set the volume

üìê The Logic: Content First, Volume Second

The order matters because:

  • Slip Audio changes which part of the audio you hear within the clip. You're choosing the right content ‚Äî the right take, the right word, the right section of background music.
  • Raise/Lower Audio changes how loud that content plays. There's no point adjusting volume on audio you might slip away.
  • If you set volume before slipping, you'd have to redo the levels after every slip adjustment ‚Äî wasted work.
  • This mirrors the professional audio workflow: select ‚Üí position ‚Üí level. The Stream Deck + knob layout follows this same sequence left to right.
üí° Think of it this way: Slip Audio = "play the right part" ‚Üí Raise/Lower Audio = "play it at the right volume." You don't set the volume on a song until you've picked which song to play.

  2. 2

  1. 3

    In Stream Deck software, click on a dial slot (recommended: Knob 3, after Slip Audio on Knob 2).
  2. 4

    Assign Click Action
    Set Press to send Ctrl+Alt+0 ‚Äî resets clip audio back to 0 dB (original level).

üí° Pro Tips

üí° Tip: DaVinci Resolve shows a horizontal volume line on each audio clip in the timeline. As you rotate the knob, you'll see this line move up or down in real time ‚Äî instant visual feedback.
üí° Tip: Select multiple audio clips before adjusting ‚Äî the volume change applies to all selected clips simultaneously. Great for batch-leveling an entire interview.
üí° Tip: Use the long press action on the knob to toggle between 1 dB and 3 dB increments ‚Äî coarse adjustment for big changes, fine adjustment for precision.
‚ö†Ô∏è Note: This adjusts clip-level gain, not the track fader in the mixer. Clip gain is applied before any track-level processing. For final mix balance, use the Fairlight page.
‚ö†Ô∏è Watch for clipping: Raising audio above 0 dB can cause distortion. Keep an eye on the audio meters ‚Äî if they hit red, lower the level.
