üîç What Is "Box Outside"?

In DaVinci Resolve's Trim Edit Mode (keyboard shortcut T), the timeline shows a bounding box around each edit point. The behaviour of a trim operation changes depending on where your mouse cursor is relative to that box:

‚úÖ Inside the Box

Cursor is over the edit-point bounding box. Clicking here activates Ripple, Roll, or Slide/Slip trim depending on exact position.

‚ùå Outside the Box

Cursor is away from the edit-point box. Clicking here deselects the current trim and may select a different edit point or do nothing ‚Äî preventing accidental edits.

Key Concept: The "outside" state is a safety mechanism. It tells the editor that the mouse is not hovering over any active trim zone, so no trim operation will be applied on click.

üí° Why Is It Used?

üõ°Ô∏è

Prevents Accidental Trims

Clicking outside the box ensures you don't accidentally ripple or roll an edit point. This safeguards your timeline from unintended changes.

üîÄ

Quick Edit-Point Switching

Clicking outside one box and onto another lets you rapidly jump between edit points without leaving Trim Mode.

üñ±Ô∏è

Cursor Feedback

The cursor icon changes when moving in and out of the box, giving instant visual feedback about which trim operation will fire.

‚ö°

Workflow Speed

Knowing the inside/outside boundary lets experienced editors work faster ‚Äî they can trust that stray clicks won't damage the edit.

üéØ How to Use in Practice

Step 1 ‚Äì Press T to enter Trim Edit Mode in DaVinci Resolve.
Step 2 ‚Äì Hover your cursor over the timeline. Notice the bounding box that appears around nearby edit points.
Step 3 ‚Äì Move the cursor inside the box to see it highlight. The cursor icon changes to indicate Ripple, Roll, or Slip.
Step 4 ‚Äì Move the cursor outside the box. The highlight disappears and the cursor returns to the default arrow ‚Äî this is the "Box Outside" state.
Step 5 ‚Äì Click while outside to deselect the current edit point, or click on a different edit point's box to switch targets.

üí° Pro Tip

Use the outside zone intentionally to "park" your cursor when you want to review the edit without risk of changing anything, then move back inside only when you're ready to trim.
