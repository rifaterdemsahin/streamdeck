üîç What Is the Yellow Timeline?

‚ö° The Live Cut Preview Indicator

When you enter Dynamic Trim Mode and begin trimming an edit point, the timeline ruler turns yellow. This yellow bar is Resolve's visual signal that live cut preview (also called "looping playback") is active around the edit point.

Visual Comparison

Normal timeline ruler (grey/blue)
Yellow timeline ruler during trim preview

The yellow colour covers the section of the timeline being looped for the cut preview.

As soon as you click and drag (or use keyboard nudge) to adjust the edit point, DaVinci Resolve automatically starts looping playback across the cut to let you see and hear the result of your trim in real time ‚Äî without pressing play.

‚öôÔ∏è How It Works

Step-by-Step Flow

Step 1 ‚Äì Press T to enter Trim Mode on the Edit page.
Step 2 ‚Äì Click on an edit point (the junction between two clips). Resolve selects the trim and highlights it.
Step 3 ‚Äì Begin trimming (drag, or press , / . to nudge). The timeline ruler turns yellow and looping playback starts automatically.
Step 4 ‚Äì The viewer shows the live result of the cut ‚Äî the last frames of the outgoing clip followed by the first frames of the incoming clip, looping continuously.
Step 5 ‚Äì Continue tweaking. Each nudge updates the preview in real time.
Step 6 ‚Äì When satisfied, click away from the edit point or press Escape. The yellow bar disappears and the timeline returns to normal.

üí° Pro Tip ‚Äì Pre-Roll & Post-Roll

DaVinci Resolve uses a configurable pre-roll and post-roll duration for the loop. By default it plays roughly 2 seconds before and after the edit point. You can adjust this under Preferences ‚Üí User ‚Üí Editing ‚Üí Pre/Post Roll to see more or less context around each cut.
